Apps

Five apps. One subscription. Infinite potential typos.

Each app is a native desktop window launched from the nOffice shell. They share data, an AI assistant, and a questionable sense of humor.

nWrite

Word processor for people who type things. Tables, images, footnotes, comments, track changes. Export to PDF or DOCX. Also supports words.

nSheet

Spreadsheet where numbers go to have meetings. HyperFormula engine, millions of rows, charts, pivot tables. AI can write your formulas so you don't have to google them.

nSlides

Presentations. For when you need to communicate ideas to a room of people who would rather be elsewhere. Transitions, animations, export to PDF and PPTX.

nImg

Image editor with layers, brushes, shapes, and filters. Also generates images from text descriptions, which means you can finally create that concept art of a raccoon in a tuxedo.

nCode

IDE based on the same editor VS Code uses (Monaco). LSP support for Python, Rust, Go, C++, Ruby. AI can explain, refactor, or generate code. May occasionally suggest adding more blockchain.

Built-in AI

Every app has an AI sidebar. Chat, rewrite, summarize, translate, generate, explain. Runs locally on a Qwen3 8B model. It's like having a coworker who never takes vacation and occasionally hallucinates.

AI

Local intelligence. Zero chill.

A Qwen3 8B model runs on your machine via llama.cpp. 32K context window, GPU accelerated, fully offline. Your data never leaves your computer, not even to ask for directions.

◆

6 context modes

Free chat, edit selection, explain, generate, summarize, translate. The AI switches between modes faster than you switch between browser tabs.

⚙

Embedding pipeline

Documents get indexed in the background via a separate embedding model. Your computer builds a searchable memory of everything you've written. It remembers things you forgot. It's already judging your spelling.

▲

Image generation

nImg connects to Stable Diffusion for txt2img and img2img. Generate images, edit with inpainting, expand with outpainting. Finally, a way to visualize "a katana-wielding pineapple surfing a tsunami."

☐

GPU goes brrr

GPU acceleration via Metal, CUDA, ROCm, or Vulkan. The model will use whatever graphics card you throw at it. Intel HD Graphics users: we respect your courage.

✕

Zero telemetry

No analytics, no tracking, no prompt logging. We have no idea what you're doing with that image generator and we're choosing not to ask.

🔒

Fully offline

Once the model downloads, everything works without internet. No cloud, no servers, no "please wait while we connect." Just you, your machine, and a sufficiently large language model.
